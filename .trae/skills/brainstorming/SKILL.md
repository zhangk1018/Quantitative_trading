# Brainstorming

This skill facilitates creative exploration and requirements gathering before implementing any feature or solution.

## Core Principles

### 1. Discovery Phase
- Understand user goals and motivations
- Identify pain points and challenges
- Explore potential solutions and alternatives

### 2. Requirements Gathering
- Collect functional requirements
- Define non-functional requirements
- Establish success criteria

### 3. Design Exploration
- Explore different approaches and architectures
- Consider technical constraints
- Evaluate trade-offs

## Brainstorming Techniques

### Mind Mapping
Visualize ideas and their relationships to identify connections and patterns.

### SWOT Analysis
Evaluate Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, and Threats for proposed solutions.

### User Story Mapping
Organize user stories and prioritize features based on user value.

### Impact-Effort Matrix
Prioritize tasks based on their impact and effort required.

## Questions to Ask

1. What is the primary goal?
2. Who are the users/stakeholders?
3. What problems are we trying to solve?
4. What are the success metrics?
5. What constraints exist?
6. What alternatives have been considered?

## Checklist

- [ ] User goals are clearly understood
- [ ] Requirements are documented
- [ ] Success criteria are defined
- [ ] Stakeholders are identified
- [ ] Constraints are documented
- [ ] Alternatives have been explored

## Output Deliverables

- Requirements document
- User stories
- Prioritized backlog
- Design sketches/concepts
- Risk assessment
